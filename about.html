My name is Shuzhan Fan, a Phd in Geographic Information Science at Louisiana State University. I also have a Master's degree in computer science. I consider myself as a data science researcher, as I play with data almost everyday. My Phd research is studying the application of deep learning in spatial-temporal crime forecasting, based on diverse multimodal data sets (e.g. crime, social media, demographic, socioeconomic, meteorologic, etc.). I also worked as a research assistant at LSU's Earth Scan Lab. My routine duties in the lab included writing codes to develop new satellite image products, as well as running and maintaining scripts to process satellite data and create images. Currently I'm a data science fellow at Insight Data Science (in San Francsico). At Insight, I developed a tool called Pho2Cap, which could automatically generate a descriptive caption of an input photo, by building an encoder-decoder deep neural networks prediction model.
